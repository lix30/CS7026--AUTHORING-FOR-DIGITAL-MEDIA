Your Guide to the Buildings of Venice

Doge's Palace

The Doge's Palace, or Palazzo Ducale, is a renowned Gothic architectural masterpiece in Venice, Italy. Historically, it served as the residence of the Doge—the chief magistrate—and the seat of the Venetian government ​

Ca' d'Oro

Ca' d'Oro, or "Golden House," is a Venetian Gothic palace on the Grand Canal in Venice, Italy. Built in the 15th century, it is renowned for its intricate façade and was once adorned with gold. Today, it houses the Galleria Giorgio Franchetti, showcasing art and sculptures

Marciana Library

The Marciana Library, or Biblioteca Nazionale Marciana, is a historic library in Venice, Italy, housing rare manuscripts and classical texts. Designed by Jacopo Sansovino in the 16th century, it is an architectural masterpiece of the Venetian Renaissance, located in Piazza San Marco

Saint Mark's Basilica

Saint Mark's Basilica (Basilica di San Marco) is a stunning cathedral in Venice, Italy, known for its Byzantine architecture and ornate mosaics. Completed in the 11th century, it was originally built to house the relics of Saint Mark
