Doge’s Palace

“The Heart of Venetian Power and Intrigue”

Listen to Guide

A Fortress of Political Might

The Doge’s Palace (Palazzo Ducale) is the embodiment of Venice’s power, both political and economic.

For centuries, it was the seat of the Doge, the elected ruler of Venice, and the home of the Venetian government. It also housed the council chambers, the court of justice, and even the prisons for those who opposed the Republic.

Facts

  • Marino Faliero: The only Doge executed for treason—his portrait was erased in shame.
  • Francesco Foscari: Forced to resign after his son was accused of treason.
  • Enrico Dandolo: A blind but brilliant Doge who led the sack of Constantinople.

Characters

  • Casanova’s Escape: He escaped from the attic prison using a metal spike.
  • Bridge of Sighs: Prisoners saw Venice for the last time from here before sentencing.

Architectural Marvels & Floor Guide

  • Golden Staircase: Grand entrance for VIPs.
  • Floating Foundation: Built on wooden piles over water.
  • Secret Passages: Rulers moved unseen through hidden doors.

Areas

  • Courtyard: With a ceremonial staircase.
  • State Rooms: Includes the massive Chamber of the Great Council.
  • Prisons: Eerie dungeons and attic cells.
  • Accessibility: Most areas are accessible, except old prisons.
